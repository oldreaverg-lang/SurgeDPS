0. Version History & Methodology Evolution
v1 — Original Exponential Depth Model
Exponential depth distribution with simple depth × occupancy lookup. MAPE: 63%, 2/6 storms in range. Wind damage: not modeled.
v2 — Rayleigh Depth + Phase 1 Wind (Previous Audit)
Replaced exponential with Rayleigh distribution (σ = peak/4.5). Phase 1 wind from peak eye-wall wind with spatial decay. MAPE: 20%, 4/6 storms in range, Wind MAPE: 99% (limited field).
v3 — Asymmetric Holland Wind Fields (Current)
Wind model upgraded: asymmetric Holland vortex with IBTrACS quadrant radii, gust factor 1.25, 50% interaction term for co-located surge-wind. MAPE: 25.4%, 3/6 storms in range, Wind MAPE: 52.3%, 3/6 wind storms within ±50%.

1b. Wind Damage Component: Model vs Real-World NEW v3

Wind Damage Comparison (Model vs Real)

Wind Model (v3): Asymmetric Holland vortex field derived from IBTrACS quadrant radii (RMW, R25, R35 per quadrant), gust factor 1.25, 50% interaction factor for combined surge-wind zones. This captures wind field asymmetry and the phenomenon where wind damage extends beyond the surge footprint. Model accurately produces $0 for Cat 1 (Sandy) and scales appropriately with intensity. Note: Wind damage currently underestimated at 52.3% MAPE; improvement will require refining IBTrACS quadrant radius calibration and gust factor tuning per basin.
2. Variance Decomposition — Why Modeled ≠ Real

Storm-by-Storm Reconciliation Table

Methodology: Per Finance plugin Variance Analysis framework. Each storm's modeled surge loss is compared to published NOAA/FEMA surge-only estimates. Variances are decomposed into: (1) depth model uncertainty (±30%), (2) building count estimation error, (3) occupancy mix effects from HAZUS Table 5.7 ratios, (4) peril interactions (surge-wind co-location). "Materiality" threshold set at 20% of benchmark per reconciliation best practices. v3 adds wind damage component separately; combined loss is primary + 30% secondary (interaction factor) to avoid double-counting.
3. Loss Waterfall — Total Hurricane Damage Allocation

Damage by Peril (All 6 Storms)

What SurgeDPS Models vs What It Doesn't

Key Finding: Storm surge represents 15–65% of total hurricane losses depending on the storm. SurgeDPS now models both surge and wind damage (via asymmetric Holland fields). Inland rainfall flooding remains not modeled. For storms like Harvey (rain-dominant), surge and wind together still represent only ~20% of total loss. This transparently shows which storms are within SurgeDPS scope vs which require additional models.

5. FEMA IHP Eligibility Analysis NEW

Estimated IHP Awards by Storm

IHP Scenario Detail

IHP Methodology: FEMA Individual and Households Program provides up to $42,500 (FY2025 max) for owner-occupied primary residences. Eligibility is estimated from modeled structural loss: minor (<$5K loss, 80% payout rate), moderate ($5K–$20K, 70%), major ($20K–$50K, 60%), severe (>$50K, capped at max). Commercial/industrial buildings are excluded. This is a modeled estimate only — actual IHP awards depend on FEMA inspection and insurance coverage.
